01 — Brand

Who we are, on a page.

AI Qadam isn't a brand bolted on top of a project — it's the visible edge of a community of practitioners building AI in Central Asia. Everything in this system serves that. Qadam means a step forward in the Turkic languages; the mark is one.

Theme-adaptive SVG — heads up.

The full lockup carries a theme switch inside the file: letters render dark on light surfaces, off-white on dark; the footprint stays teal. The download is the same file in both themes — what you see depends on where you open it (browser, Figma, Preview each render the fallback differently). For print, partner decks, or any context that needs a fixed colour, open the SVG in Figma or Illustrator and bake the .aiq-logo-dark fill to the colour you need before exporting.

Don't

✕

Don't recolour. The mark is the brand teal — anything else reads as a different organisation.

✕

Don't stretch, skew, or rotate. Use the SVG; it scales without loss.

✕

Don't add shadows, glows, gradients, or outlines. The mark is flat.

✕

Don't crop the four dots. They're part of the silhouette, not decoration.

✕

Don't substitute the wordmark with a different typeface — it is the SVG, not Geist.

✕

Don't place the mark on busy imagery. Let it sit on a flat surface with breathing room.

Voice & principles

Seven principles from the community manifesto. Copy, design and product decisions all answer to these — when in doubt, pick the option that honours more of them.

01

Honesty over hype

No promoting what doesn't exist or lacks real expert backing. Honesty over loud promises.

02

Practice over theory

Speakers share what they did themselves. A real case beats a polished framework.

03

Quality over reach

We measure usefulness, not turnout. 80 engaged people matter more than 500 indifferent ones.

04

The right to fail

We talk about failures openly. An honest post-mortem teaches more than a success story.

05

Community, not channel

Partners invest in people and the community. We're not an advertising venue.

06

Multilingual by default

We speak the languages of the region — state languages, Russian, English — and respect each member's choice.

07

People first

The community exists for the benefit of its participants, not for monetising them through outside interests.

Writing the name

Small rules, applied consistently.

In prose

AI Qadam

Title case. Two words. The Q in Qadam is hard, like in Qazaq.

In the lockup

AI QADAM

All caps, locked together with the mark. Only inside the logo — never in body copy.

Hashtag

#AIQadam

One token, camel case. Used on every public post.

Don't

AI-Qadam · AIQadam · ai qadam · AI Kadam

Hyphens, mashed-up casing, lowercase and phonetic spellings are all out.

03 — Typography

Three families, one voice.

Geist for display (titles, hero, brand). Inter for everything you read in a paragraph. JetBrains Mono for technical detail (times, IDs, tags). Both Cyrillic and Latin must read equally well — Central Asia uses both.

Type scale

Geist (display) · Inter (body) · JetBrains Mono (technical). Both scripts must read equally well.

04 — Photography

Real rooms, real people.

Photography for AI Qadam is documentary, not staged. Real speakers mid-sentence, real laptops, real Q&A hands going up. Avoid stock-AI tropes (blue circuit brains, white robots, glowing data orbs). The honesty principle applies to images too.

Don't

✕

No AI-generated humans, robots, brain graphics, or glowing-data backgrounds.

✕

No corporate handshake shots, staged smiles, or empty boardroom posing.

✕

No heavy filters, vignettes, or duotone overlays — keep the colour honest.

✕

No watermarks. Photographer credit goes in caption, not on the image.

05 — Iconography

Lucide line · brand teal for accents.

Product UI uses Lucide at 2px stroke, 24×24 viewBox. For brand surfaces (decks, merch, posters) the footprint-with-dots motif from the logo is the only allowed pictogram — never duplicate it; never substitute it.

06 — Merch templates

Wearables, swag, print.

What AI Qadam chapters hand out at events: a shopper, a notebook, a pen. Funded by partners as a community contribution, distributed free at meetups — never sold.

Want your own AI Qadam merch — for your chapter, your study group, a personal tote? Go ahead. We don't restrict the format; we only ask that the identity travels intact. Non-commercial use only — selling anything that carries the mark, the wordmark, or the brand teal isn't allowed, by anyone. See the Brand Usage Policy.

Making your own?

Use the SVG straight from brand/logo-full.svg or brand/logo-mark.svg — no redrawing, no recolouring, no AI-generated variants. Brand teal prints as Pantone 7716 C for safe colour-matching across suppliers. Paying a vendor to produce a one-off is fine; selling the result isn't — the bag is free, the brand isn't for sale.

07 — Decks & speaker templates

Speakers, fork this.

Two starting points for anyone speaking at an AI Qadam meetup: a PPTX you can open in PowerPoint / Keynote / Google Slides, and an HTML deck that's editable in any text editor — and AI-friendly so you can ask Claude/ChatGPT to draft your slides directly into it.

Both share the same six-slide skeleton: title · speaker bio · section divider · content · quote · closing. Brand visual language (teal, four-dot motif, wordmark) is baked in. Don't override.

House rules for any deck

✕

Don't recolour the wordmark or the four dots. Teal lives on --primary; everything else is neutral.

✕

Don't use AI-generated imagery. Photos must be real — see Photography.

✕

Don't substitute the display family. Geist for headings, Inter for body, JetBrains Mono for meta.

✕

Don't bury the speaker. Name + role on slide 2 is non-negotiable.

✕

Don't pitch a product on a community meetup deck. See Brand Usage Policy — partners contribute, they don't advertise.

✕

Don't drift the eyebrow. Always AI Qadam · <context> in JetBrains Mono caps.
